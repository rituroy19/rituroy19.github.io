World's Busiest Airports

An insight into the biggest airports of planet Earth.

The Aviation Industry is booming!


The net worth of the industry is around 35.5 billion US Dollars. In 2009, the aviation industry entered a golden age. Between 2009 and 2017, the revenue grew at a compund annual growth rate of 5.9% reaching 754 billion US Dollars in 2017.


The annual growth rate of the number of passengers across airports is 3.5% compunded annually. It was around 1.467 billion passengers in 1998, around 3.979 billion passengers in 2017 and just this year it reached close to 9 billion passenegers per year (6.4% growth)!

A lens through the time


Since 1970, less than 500,000 passengers used to travel via air. Now, you can see that number reach 4 billion passengers per year in 2018. This is exciting to see the dynamic change in the industry through this lens. Quite possibly, there can be some really interesting patterns of growth in different aspects.

Busiest Airports of all time


Let's look at some of the busiest airports of the world since 2000 and how dramatically they have been changing. US clearly was the forerunner with majority passengers in the earlier years.

Over the years, Asian countries have seen the majority growth with countries like China and UAE offering most flights to majority passengers at cheaper rates!

Growth has fairly been constant for European countries. However, Atlanta airport remains to be at the top through the years, having a growth of about 3% passenegrs per year. It's still growing and is still unbeaten!

Connectedness of Airports


It is interesting to compare the connectedness of each airport. London remains to be the most connected, which makes sense as it is such centrally located on the world map. However, their growth has been sort of constant.

Atlanta on the other hand has constantly been growing in terms of passenger capacity and ranks the top to be the busiest airport in the world for over 20 years. But it is not as connected as its European counter-parts.
